Lightsaber
Rice Krispie Treats

An idiot-proof recipe for two

20 Min Prep
10 Min Set
8–10 Sabers
⬡ Supplies
🥣
Rice Krispies 6 cups
☁️
Marshmallows 1 bag (10 oz)
🧈
Butter 3 tablespoons
🥨
Pretzel rods 8–10 rods
🔵
Blue candy melts 1 bag
🔴
Red candy melts 1 bag
⬡ Mission Briefing
01
⚠ Melt the Butter
Put a large pot on the stove over low heat. Add the 3 tablespoons of butter. Let it melt slowly — don't rush this with high heat or it'll burn.
Low and slow is the move. Burnt butter smells bad and ruins everything.
02
⚠ Add Marshmallows
Pour the entire bag of marshmallows into the pot. Stir constantly with a wooden spoon or spatula. Keep heat on low. They'll melt in about 3–4 minutes. Keep stirring until completely smooth — no lumps.
The second they're fully melted, pull the pot off the heat. Overcooked marshmallow = hard treats.
03
Add the Cereal
Take the pot off the heat. Pour in all 6 cups of Rice Krispies. Stir quickly until every piece is coated. Work fast — it gets harder to mix as it cools.
04
Shape the Blades
Spray a 9x13 pan or baking sheet with cooking spray. Dump the mixture in. Wet your hands slightly (stops sticking) and press it into an even layer about 1 inch thick. Let it cool for 5 minutes.
Don't press too hard — lighter pressure = softer treats.
05
Cut Into Rectangles
Once cooled, cut into long rectangles — about 1 inch wide and 4–5 inches long. These are your lightsaber blades. You should get 8–10 pieces. A pizza cutter works great here if you have one.
06
⚠ Melt the Candy Melts
Do each color separately. Pour half a bag of blue melts into a microwave-safe bowl. Microwave in 30-second bursts, stirring between each one. Usually takes 2–3 rounds. Repeat with red in a separate bowl. The chocolate should be smooth and drizzle-able — not thick.
If it's too thick, add half a teaspoon of vegetable oil and stir. Don't overheat or it seizes up.
07
Assemble the Sabers
Press a pretzel rod into one end of each treat rectangle — this is the handle. Then dip or drizzle the treat part in your color of choice. Blue for Jedi. Red for Sith. Do half and half so you each have your own side.
Dipping = full coverage. Drizzling = messier but more fun to do together.
08
Let Them Set
Lay the finished sabers on parchment paper or a wire rack. They'll be ready in 5–10 minutes at room temperature, or pop them in the fridge for 3 minutes if you're impatient.
09
Duel (or Eat)
Hold them up. Make the sound. Take a photo. Then eat them. You're done.
